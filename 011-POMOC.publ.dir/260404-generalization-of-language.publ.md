what is common in all of these?



- they are all systems of symbols and rules for manipulating them
- they all have a syntax and semantics
- they all have a purpose of communication or expression
- they all have a structure that can be analyzed and understood


- clause-like structures
  - SVOMPT-like
  - SVO-like
  - SOV-like

- phrase-like structures
  - noun-phrase-like structures
    - possibly path-like structures
  - verb-like structures

- modifiers

- time-like structures


- they all have a way of generating new expressions from existing ones
- they all have a way of interpreting expressions to produce meaning or action
- they all have a way of evolving and adapting over time
- they all have a way of being learned and used by agents (humans or machines)
- they all have a way of being shared and transmitted among agents
- they all have a way of being used for different domains and contexts
- they all have a way of being used for different purposes and goals
- they all have a way of being used for different levels of abstraction and complexity
- they all have a way of being used for different modalities and media
- they all have a way of being used for different types of communication (e.g. interpersonal, intrapersonal, mass, etc.)
- they all have a way of being used for different types of expression (e.g. descriptive, prescriptive, performative, etc.)
- they all all have a way of being used for different types of reasoning (e.g. deductive, inductive, abductive, etc.)

- they all have a way of being used for different types of creativity (e.g. combinatorial, exploratory, transformational, etc.)
